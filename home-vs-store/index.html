Homemade VS Commercial
Baked Goods

We compared the different nutrient intakes between homemade baked goods and commercial baked goods. This comparison serves as a tool to show that baking your own goods is, more often than not, beneficial. Note: Each intake is based on a proportional serving size, specified in the excel

We created a table of a sample, then turned that table into two box plots (homemade baked goods and commercial baked goods). This was done in order to compare the spread of the proteins-carbohydrates-fats-fibers in each group, respectively. Then, we create a table that may ensue with a T-Test to calculate the significance of the difference between the two types of baked goods. And finally, with the results create pie charts that will serve as visual comparisons the distinct proportions between the homemade and commercial baked goods.
